# Case card copy

Working draft for portfolio case-card copy. Russian copy currently captures the editorial intent; English copy should adapt that intent into natural portfolio English, not translate it word for word.

## Card copy principle

Case cards are not role descriptions. They should help a hiring manager decide which case to open.

Each card should describe:

1. what kind of product or scenario this is;
2. what makes the task specific or complex;
3. why this case is relevant for a product-design role.

Avoid starting every card with “Designed”, “Created”, “Спроектировал” or “Создал”. The work is design by definition; the card should sell the case, not repeat the job title.

English and Russian copy are written separately. English should sound like natural portfolio English. Russian should sound like natural Russian product/design writing, not a translation.

Punctuation rule for cards: keep one-sentence descriptions without a final period. If a card needs two or more sentences, use periods consistently in every sentence.

Terminology note: use “food hall” for a contemporary venue with many independent food vendors or restaurant corners. “Food court” sounds more like a mall cafeteria. “Market hall” is closer to a public market or food market.

## Conclusions from the Russian copy

- The card should name the artifact first: analytics, ordering flow, stories, website, promo site, platform, magazine, app.
- The value should come from specificity, not from role verbs. “Спроектировал” is useful in a case body, not as the default card opening.
- Russian and English can use different syntax. The Russian version can be more direct and object-led; the English version can use concise portfolio phrasing if it keeps the same meaning.
- Avoid subjective praise and vague elevation: no “beautiful design”, “premium experience”, “visual world”, “reducing uncertainty” or “friction”.
- When the artifact is brand-led, describe what the site connects or reveals, not that the case is “visual”.

## Editorial notes

- Samokat should not start with “Samokat is #1…” on the card. The card is about the site and what it explains, not a company profile.
- “Public website” is redundant for Sevcableport unless the contrast is with an internal tool. “Website” is enough.
- “Reducing uncertainty” is too abstract for PYE. Say what the experience lets a person do before buying.
- “Reducing friction” is generic product-speak. For Big Orders, say what the flow is for: courier pickup of large, heavy and oversized orders.
- Keep “Designed and built” for AVGVST only if both design and build are true public claims. Otherwise describe the artifact and experience, not the case type or role.
- “Campaign pages” may be correct in English only if the project really had a reusable campaign-page system. Do not use “кампейн-страницы” in Russian.
- For AVGVST, describe the artifact first: a jewelry brand website that connects shopping with storytelling about production and brand character. Do not call the artifact “a visual case” inside the card.
- In Russian, avoid “мобильный опыт”. Say “приложение”, “сценарий” or “выбор и примерка”.
- Write “Севкабельпорт” as one word in Russian.
